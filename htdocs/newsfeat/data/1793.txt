LOS ANGELES — One person was killed at a Trader Joe’s in Los Angeles on Saturday, when a gunman exchanged fire with the police, ran into the store and barricaded himself with hostages for about three hours before surrendering, city officials said.The victim’s family identified her on social media as Melyda Corado, an employee at the store. In a statement on Sunday, Trader Joe’s said the tragedy was “the saddest day in Trader Joe’s history as we mourn the loss of one of our own.”The Los Angeles Police Department identified the suspect as Gene Atkins, 28. A police spokesman said Mr. Atkins had been charged with murder. Records show he was being held with bail set at $2 million.

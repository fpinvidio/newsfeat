Back in 1992 the food writer Molly O’Neill playfully named David Firestone “the Latke King” in her “New York Cookbook,” and included a recipe he had modified from his mother’s and grandmother’s. Mr. Firestone, who was for many years an editor at The Times, now uses a food processor to make big batches, sometimes just for the family, sometimes for 60 friends or more. As for the olive oil, it may be the liquid symbol of Hanukkah, but in his house it’s imported from Italy.Featured in: Holidays Holding Hands.

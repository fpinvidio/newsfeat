    Your Midterm Pop Quiz

    By GAIL COLLINS NOV. 2, 2018

    Sure North Dakota has a Senate seat. You knew that.

    Midterm elections on Tuesday! Truly, people, have you ever before gotten so wound up about races for Congress in districts 1,000 miles from your home? Did you even know North Dakota had a Senate seat?

    Well, this year we’re focused as all get out. Let’s see how much knowledge you’ve picked up.

    1 of 14

    In Pennsylvania, Scott Wagner, the Republican candidate for governor, produced a video in which he warns his opponent, Tom Wolf, to put on a “catcher’s mask” because:

    “I’m going to toss so many plans for deficit reduction at you, your head will spin.”

    “I’m going to stomp all over your face with golf spikes.”

    “It’s time to play charity softball!”

    2 of 14

    In Arizona, six of Republican Representative Paul Gosar’s siblings made a campaign ad in which they …

    Reminisced fondly that Paul used to read “The Conscience of a Conservative” to them at bedtime.

    Denounced Democratic rumors that as a boy, Gosar cheated at Monopoly.

    Endorsed their brother’s opponent.

    3 of 14

    At a rally in Mississippi, President Trump reminded voters that he was not on the ballot, then added:

    “Pretend I’m on the ballot.”

    “Let me introduce your great Senate candidate … um, whatsisname.”

    “Have I mentioned immigration?”

    4 of 14

    Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton, a Republican, is a favorite for re-election despite a series of, um, embarrassments, including an indictment on two counts of securities fraud and charges that he …

    Stole another lawyer’s $1,000 pen.

    Kicked a puppy.

    Really likes Beto O’Rourke.

    5 of 14

    Senator Robert Menendez’s re-election campaign is in more trouble than you’d expect for a Democrat in New Jersey. The problem seems to be Menendez’s …

    Unflattering eyeglasses.

    Habit of taking expensive presents and trips from a campaign contributor who had business with the government.

    Comment on Twitter that his favorite weekend getaway was “Anyplace but Trenton.”

    6 of 14

    Republican Congressman Duncan Hunter of California is running for re-election despite being indicted on a charge of using $250,000 in campaign contributions for personal expenses that included everything from dental bills to entertaining his mistresses. He’s arguing that his opponent…

    “Could use some serious teeth-cleaning himself.”

    “Would do all that stuff too, if he had $250,000 in campaign contributions.”

    Is an Islamic terrorist sympathizer “working to infiltrate Congress.”

    7 of 14

    When Democrat Joe Manchin first ran for Senate, his campaign featured a famous ad that showed him shooting a proposal to reduce global warming. This time, he’s back with his rifle, putting a hole in …

    A “Save the Polar Bear” poster.

    Gun control legislation.

    A lawsuit to stop Obamacare.

    8 of 14

    The big North Dakota Senate race has focused on the vote by Heidi Heitkamp, a Democrat, against Brett Kavanaugh for Supreme Court. Her opponent, Kevin Cramer, claimed that his wife and daughters can’t understand the #MeToo movement because …

    “I censor their TV time.”

    They’re “tough people whose grandparents were tough and great-grandparents were tough.”

    “It’s not a North Dakota kind of thing.”

    9 of 14

    Jim Renacci, the Ohio Republican Senate candidate, claimed he’s inspired President Trump to be more unifying by …

    Introducing a compromise tax reform bill with Democratic colleagues.

    Helping colleagues write campaign speeches that don’t mention Nancy Pelosi.

    Hosting a “bipartisan breakfast group.”

    10 of 14

    In Missouri, Democratic Senator Claire McCaskill has been assuring voters she’s …

    “Not one of those crazy candidates.”

    “Not one of those crazy Democrats.”

    “Not one of those crazy former state auditors.”

    11 of 14

    While Alaska is not exactly in the vicinity of Mexico, the state’s lone congressman, Don Young, a Republican, is running on the “caravan” issue. When the marchers reach the border, he told a debate audience:

    “That’s going to be the big, big enchilada.”

    “That’s going to be the big, big moose steak.”

    “That’s going to be a time when we all have to come together and get serious about solving the problem.”

    12 of 14

    In the Buffalo area, Representative Chris Collins is running for re-election while he’s under federal indictment for security fraud and insider trading. On his website, Collins has a “Values” page that includes a section on “Personal Accountability.” He defines personal accountability as …

    “Never increasing entitlement programs.”

    “Never giving census takers the wrong address.”

    “Never serving on a committee that oversees the pharmaceutical business when you have big investments in pharmaceutical company stocks.”

    13 of 14

    Election season is the time when long-ago comments suddenly come back to haunt you. In Arizona, Representative Kyrsten Sinema, the Democratic senate candidate, keeps being reminded that in a 2011 address to Netroots Nation, she called her home state …

    “Really nice although a little underwatered.”

    “The meth lab of democracy.”

    “A place where the bola tie is the official state neckwear.”

    14 of 14

    The Florida Republican candidate for governor, Ron DeSantis, now says that contrary to his campaign ad, he doesn’t actually …

    Drink a glass of red algae every night at dinner to demonstrate his confidence in Florida water.

    Read his infant son bedtime stories from “The Art of the Deal.”

    Have a pet python named Hillary.

    Gail Collins is an Op-Ed columnist, a former member of the editorial board and was the first woman to serve as Times editorial page editor, from 2001 to 2007.

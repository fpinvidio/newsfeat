    What You Need to Know About Guns in America

    By DANIEL VICTOR JAN. 7, 2016

    This collection of past Times interactive graphics and explanatory articles focuses on the American gun control debate.


    1. Gun Control Explained

      Some of the most frequently asked questions about gun control are answered, including the current laws, common arguments for and against, and why there is little political appetite to pass sweeping new laws.


    2. How They Got Their Guns

      A look at how the gunmen in several recent mass shootings acquired their weapons shows that the vast majority of the guns used were bought legally and with a federal background check. Several of the gunmen had criminal histories or documented mental health problems.





    3. How Gun Traffickers Get Around State Gun Laws

      A thriving underground market for guns has significantly diluted the effect of state-specific gun laws. The New York Times examined gun trafficking by analyzing nine years of data, showing how guns move freely on their way to criminals.

    4. State Gun Laws Enacted in the Year After Newtown

      In the year after the shooting at Sandy Hook Elementary School in Connecticut in 2012, 109 new state gun laws were passed, 70 of which loosened gun restrictions. Almost every state passed at least one new gun law.

    6. Presidential Candidates on Gun Violence

      All of the Democratic presidential candidates favor new gun laws, while nearly all of the Republican candidates are against additional restrictions. We include some of the candidates’ most notable quotations on the subject.

    7. Recurring Sorrow: President Obama’s Remarks After Gun Violence

      A review of President Obama’s statements after several notable mass shootings shows his language has changed little throughout his presidency.

      “We can’t tolerate this anymore. These tragedies must end. And to end them, we must change,” he said after Sandy Hook in 2012.

      “This is not normal. We can’t let it become normal,” he said after the shootings at a Planned Parenthood center in Colorado Springs in 2015.

    8. Gun Sales Soar After Obama Calls for New Restrictions

      There have been sharp spikes in gun sales after each call for stricter gun laws. Two of the largest increases came after the shootings at Sandy Hook Elementary School in 2012 and in San Bernardino, Calif., in 2015, with another large increase after President Obama was re-elected in 2008. The increases are largely a result of the increased popularity of handguns, which until recent years were far less popular than rifles and shotguns.

    9. In Other Countries, You’re as Likely to Be Killed by a Falling Object as by a Gun

      In most of the Western world, gun homicides are as rare as deaths from falling tree limbs or plane crashes. Compare the likelihood of being killed with a gun in the United States to 21 other countries, in everyday terms from a lightning strike to a heart attack.

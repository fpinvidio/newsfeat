    Mr. Trump’s already short trip was cut even shorter early Thursday afternoon when the White House announced he would remain in Washington for longer than expected. Still, the trip has the potential to cause major disruptions, including street closings and protests near the Intrepid that could bring traffic on the West Side Highway to a standstill.

    Mr. Trump was originally scheduled to arrive at J.F.K. around 3 p.m. and travel via helicopter to the Peninsula Hotel to meet with Mr. Turnbull (the hotel is on Fifth Avenue, one block south of Trump Tower, where Mr. Trump lived and worked until his inauguration). From there, the president, Melania Trump, Mr. Turnbull and his wife were going to travel by motorcade to the Intrepid.

    With Mar-a-Lago, Mr. Trump’s exclusive resort in Palm Beach, Fla., soon closing for the season, the president is expected to spend more time, including this weekend, at his New Jersey club. The centerpiece of the golf course is the clubhouse, a 1939 red brick mansion in the Georgian Revival-style where Mr. Trump hosted a procession of potential officials for his administration before his inauguration.

    Your Guide to Thanksgiving Travel

    By THE NEW YORK TIMES NOV. 24, 2015

    Tips for the biggest travel day of the year, including how to ready your car for the trip, how to get through seven major airports and which hotels outside the United States are planning their own celebrations.

    1. In Transit
      How to Navigate 7 Major Airports

      For fliers, the holidays often present lengthy airport security lines, fast-food temptations and mass scrambles for open electrical outlets. Our survival guide details how to navigate those challenges at domestic terminals at seven major airports: Chicago O’Hare International, Dallas/Fort Worth International, Denver International and Los Angeles International airports and LaGuardia and JFK in New York.

    2. Upshot
      What Search Data Tells You About Thanksgiving Air Travel

      In all, more than 3.6 million Americans — or slightly more than 1 percent of the country’s population — are expected to take a flight for Thanksgiving. Where are they going? Somewhere warm, based on The Upshot’s analysis of search data from Google Flights. This and more conclusions can be found here.

    3. Holiday Travelers Facing Tougher Security Measures

      Millions of Americans who take to the skies for the busiest travel period of the year, in the aftermath of terrorist attacks around the world, are expected to see longer security lines and more careful screening, more uniformed patrols and more bomb-sniffing dogs. But there is little sign so far that concerns about safety are keeping people home for Thanksgiving. Read more.

    4. Ways to Celebrate, Old and New

      Many families have their own traditions for Thanksgiving, whether it is an intergenerational breakfast, a game of flag football in the neighbor’s backyard, or something that takes people farther outside their homes, like a parade or a special volunteer activity. There are also a number of newer and nontraditional events in many American cities. Read more.

    6. Getaway
      13 Ways to Upgrade Your Road Trip

      While talk of Thanksgiving travel often centers around the cost and tribulations of flying, during the holiday weekend more Americans actually hit the road. This year, why not make the journey more enjoyable? After all, you could spend hours sitting in traffic. Everyone knows to bring along the usual accessories, like phone chargers and tablet mounts. But certain extras can help shift a ho-hum road trip into the next gear. Here is our guide.

    7. In Transit
      Leaving the Country? There’s Still Turkey to Be Had

      As more travelers are on the go during Thanksgiving, many global destinations are offering traditional dinners blended with regional fare. Here are some of them.

    8. A Thanksgiving Reader

      Stuck at the terminal? These stories about settlers and Indians may help you pass the time. One writer follows in the footsteps of Hannah Duston, who was victim and victor in a skirmish in a war she never meant to fight in. Another hangs out with the Indians who live at the base of the Grand Canyon and gets an unsparing look at the ways that we interact with the people who once were the sole residents of this land.

    Maps: Hurricane Florence’s Approach Toward the Carolinas

    UPDATED SEPT. 13, 2018

    View the latest maps tracking Hurricane Florence’s path, damage and impact. This page is no longer updated.

    Hurricane Florence approached the North Carolina coast on Thursday as a Category 2 storm, with winds of up to 110 miles an hour, as forecasters said that it had the potential for catastrophic, life-threatening damage and rainfall.

    Past Hurricanes in the Region

    Updated Sept. 13, 3:00 p.m.

    At least three storms have hit the region with hurricane-force winds since 2008. The largest, Hurricane Irene in 2011, had hurricane-force winds that spanned up to 135 miles when it hit the coast of North Carolina. Hurricane Florence, which is expected to make landfall on Friday, has already reached that size.

    Week of Violence Rattles Germany

    By CARYN A. WILSON JULY 26, 2016

    In a span of six days in July, four high-profile attacks occurred in Germany, putting the nation on edge. Ten people were killed and more than 40 wounded in Würzburg, Munich, Reutlingen and Ansbach. Three of the four attacks were carried out by recent immigrants, two from Syria and one from Afghanistan. The fourth attacker was an Iranian-German.

    In a span of six days in July, four high-profile attacks occurred in Germany, putting the nation on edge. Ten people were killed and more than 40 wounded in Würzburg, Munich, Reutlingen and Ansbach. Three of the four attacks were carried out by recent immigrants, two from Syria and one from Afghanistan. The fourth attacker was an Iranian-German.

    2. July 18 – Würzburg, Bavaria
      Ax Attack on Train
      Five Injured

      A 17-year-old who entered Germany as an asylum seeker last year attacked several people with an ax and a knife on a train. He fled the train, then attacked a woman walking her dog before he was killed by the police.

      The teenager was from Afghanistan and had apparently become upset after hearing the news of the death of a friend from home.

      Investigators say they believe that he was a sympathizer of the Islamic State because they found a hand-drawn flag of the militant group in the room he had at the home of his German foster family. Within hours of the attack, the group claimed responsibility and called the teenager an “Islamic State soldier.”

    3. July 22 – Munich, Bavaria
      Shooting at Shopping Mall
      Nine Dead, 21 Injured

      Nine people were killed and 21 were injured after a man armed with a handgun began shooting outside a McDonald’s before going inside the Olympia Shopping Center, described as the largest mall in Bavaria.

      The 18-year-old gunman was an Iranian-German who grew up in Munich. Officials said he was obsessed with violence and had been bullied in school.

      The gunman killed himself. When the police discovered his body, they found 300 rounds of ammunition in his backpack.

    4. July 24 – Reutlingen, Baden-Württemberg
      Knife Killing
      One Killed

      A man used a kebab knife to kill a woman with whom witnesses said he had argued earlier in the day.

      The attacker, a 21-year-old Syrian refugee, was arrested by the police after a motorist struck him with his car.

      Investigators have said that the crime was “linked to relationship troubles.”

    5. July 24 – Ansbach, Bavaria
      Explosion Near Music Festival
      Fifteen Injured

      A man killed himself and wounded 15 people by detonating an explosion at a wine bar near a music festival.

      The German authorities identified the man as a 27-year-old Syrian who was living in refugee housing although he had been denied asylum last year.

      He had apparently tried to enter the open-air music festival but was not allowed inside. Investigators said that the explosive was filled with “small metal parts.”

      The man, who had attempted suicide twice before, recorded a cellphone video in which he professed loyalty to the Islamic State, officials from Germany said.

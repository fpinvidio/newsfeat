    8. Union Leader, Out of Jail, Vows to Fight No-Strike Law

      Roger Toussaint, the president of the transit workers' union, sounded as defiant as ever after walking out of jail at 9 a.m. yesterday, having served nearly 4 days of his 10-day sentence for leading an illegal strike. Wearing a black T-shirt that read, ''It's about respect,'' he criticized the judge who sentenced him, the state law that his union violated and the chairman of the Metropolitan Transportation Authority.
      April 29, 2006

    9. Police Link Death of Girl, 9, To Problem With Shower Hose

      Detectives and child welfare workers are investigating the death on Thursday night of a 9-year-old Brooklyn girl who may have been accidentally hanged by the hose of an adjustable shower head, officials said. The girl, Gabriella Illias, was taken to Maimonides Medical Center after her mother, Elizabeth Reyes, heard her screaming about 9 p.m. and rushed into the bathroom to find her standing in the bathtub with her head tangled in the loop of the shower hose, the police said. The front of her neck was pressed against the hose, they said.
      April 29, 2006

    10. A Life Too Short, With Cruelty as Its Bookends

      Quashay Everett was a year old when her parents tortured her older sister, Teisha, in a Bronx apartment in a case of child abuse that shocked the city. At 15, Quashay's life ended in brutal anonymity, her body found stuffed into a black garbage bag in tall weeds near Kennedy Airport. The girl had been missing for almost a year, listed on law enforcement Web sites as a runaway. Two men happened upon her body on March 6. How she disappeared and what happened to her afterward is not known.
      April 28, 2006

    2. Police Link Man They Arrested to 4 Rapes in Queens Over 9 Years

      A routine traffic stop in Brooklyn this week resulted in the arrest of a man who the police say is a serial rapist who has been at large for the past 11 years, the authorities said last night. Fingerprints taken from the man, Jauan Griffith, 27, of Jamaica, Queens, after he was stopped on suspicion of reckless driving, matched those on record for the man sought in four rapes and an attempted rape in and around the Laurelton and Rosedale neighborhoods in Queens from 1995 to 2004, the police said.
      April 28, 2006

    3. That Wild Taxi Ride Is Safer Than You Think, a Study Says

      In a city where almost everyone has a story about zigzagging through traffic in a hair-raising, white-knuckled cab ride, a new traffic safety study may come as a surprise: It finds that taxis are pretty safe. So are livery cars, according to the study, which is based on state motor vehicle records of accidents and injuries across the city. It concludes that taxi and livery-cab drivers have crash rates one-third lower than drivers of other vehicles.
      April 28, 2006

    4. Murder Charge For Ex-Officer In S.I. Shooting

      As investigators sought to unravel the fatal shooting of a retired police officer in a Staten Island parking lot on Monday night, portraits emerged of the victim and the other retired police officer who was charged yesterday with the killing, and of the starkly different paths they had traveled. The victim had been hailed as a hero of the first attack on the World Trade Center and went on to the top management of a Manhattan security firm. The suspect is nine years younger but, said people who know him, lived a sedentary, lonely life, drinking alone at his home after a career marked not by valor but by pain.
      April 12, 2006

    5. As a Husband Lies Shot, His Kin Recall the Women

      Todd B. Jamison is a popular basketball coach and doting father and was much admired during his 20 years with the New York Police Department. He has a fondness for luxury cars and, according to family members and a former spouse, a weakness for women. Mr. Jamison's ways were apparently too much for his wife to bear, law enforcement officials say.
      April 12, 2006

    6. Retired Officer Is Killed In Hail of Bullets at S.I. Mall

      A retired Port Authority police officer was shot at least eight times and killed yesterday in the parking lot of a Staten Island shopping center by a retired New York City police officer as he and his wife were walking toward a Chinese restaurant, the police said. Late last night, the police had surrounded the nearby home of the suspect, who had fled in his car, and were trying to coax him to surrender.
      April 11, 2006

    7. Retired Police Officer Shot; Wife Is Held for Questioning

      A retired New York City police officer was seriously injured early yesterday when a woman drove up alongside the car he was driving on a Brooklyn street and shot him at least four times, the police said. Within hours, the victim's wife, herself a city police officer, was being held by the authorities in New Jersey in connection with the shooting. The shooting took place on a bustling weekday morning about 10 a.m. near a school in East New York when, the police said, a woman driving a silver rental car pulled alongside the victim's red Mercedes-Benz, fired a few shots, then sped to the corner.
      April 11, 2006

    8. 4 Harlem Teenagers Charged in Student's Death During Robbery

      The police have arrested four Harlem teenagers and charged them with the robbery and murder of a New York University student who died earlier this month after he was chased onto 125th Street and hit by a car. Although law enforcement officials had been investigating the incident as a possible bias crime, citing an anonymous witness who said he heard one of the attackers, who were black, yell, ''Get the white guy,'' they determined that the assault on the student, Broderick J. Hehman, was instead a robbery gone awry.
      April 10, 2006

    9. Recalling The Failings Of a Son Lost In Greenwich

      The murder of Andrew Kissel earlier this week in his house here was ''the culmination of something, of many difficult years,'' his father, William, said on Thursday as he made preparations to bury the son who had swindled banks and title companies of tens of millions of dollars in a series of real estate deals. Andrew Kissel, 46, was found stabbed to death in his basement on Monday morning, days before he was scheduled to plead guilty to federal charges of bank fraud stemming from his elaborate charades. His wife, a former world champion skier, was divorcing him and had moved out two days earlier, taking their two daughters with her.
      April 7, 2006

    10. Details of Tangled Web Emerge In Fatal Greenwich Stabbing

      Police officials investigating the stabbing death of Andrew M. Kissel, a real estate developer who had been charged with fraud, have found no signs of robbery or forced entry in the house where his body was discovered, they said on Tuesday. The fact that the killer or killers got in without using force suggests that they knew their victim, and that was one of several details released Tuesday.
      April 5, 2006

    11. Developer on Eve of Guilty Plea Is Discovered Slain at His Home

      Andrew M. Kissel, the wealthy Greenwich real estate developer who had agreed to plead guilty this week to having swindled banks, title companies and others out of tens of millions of dollars, was found dead on Monday, his hands and feet bound, in the blood-splattered basement of his home, according to the police and employees of a moving company who discovered the body. Greenwich police officers arrived about 9:45 a.m. at the home on Dairy Road that Mr. Kissel and his wife, Hayley, had been renting for three years. Officers and investigators from the state medical examiner's office spent the day combing through the house, which they sealed off with yellow crime-scene tape, as reporters and neighbors watched. The body was removed shortly after 7 p.m.
      April 4, 2006

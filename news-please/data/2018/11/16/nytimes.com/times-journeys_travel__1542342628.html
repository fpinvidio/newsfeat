New Year’s in New York is beautifully bright, and it’s even brighter when you can spend it at the Metropolitan Opera. Join Fred Plotkin, the opera and culinary expert (and native New Yorker), for four stellar productions at the Met with some of opera’s biggest stars and four of the world’s best conductors. You’ll have plenty of time to explore New York, too.

The heart of opera in the United States is the Metropolitan Opera House, in the heart of New York City. Go behind the scenes at this magnificent building and attend performances of “Otello,” a new production of “La Traviata,” the opening night gala performance of “Adriana Lecouvreur” and, to start the new year, the Mozart classic “The Magic Flute.” Scheduled performers include Anna Netrebko, Diana Damrau, Sonya Yoncheva, Piotr Beczala, Juan Diego Flórez and Nathan Gunn. Add the superstar maestros Harry Bicket, Gustavo Dudamel, Yannick Nézet-Séguin and Gianandrea Noseda, and an opera lover can find no better way to ring in 2019.

Scheduled Experts

  • Fred Plotkin

    Dec 27–Jan 2 departure
    Fred Plotkin is one of the world’s most respected opera experts and author of “Opera 101: A Complete Guide to Learning and Loving Opera.” He has lectured, taught, consulted and held managerial or artistic roles at some of the top opera companies, including La Scala and the Metropolitan Opera. He is a frequent visitor to Italy, Germany, Austria, France and Britain and has immersed himself in these countries' rich cultures.

Journey Highlights

  • Attend four performances in first-class seats at the Met, featuring four of the world’s best conductors.
  • Take a backstage tour of the magnificent Metropolitan Opera House.
  • Attend the opening night of a new production of Cilea’s “Adriana Lecouvreur.”
  • Learn about the history of New York’s opera culture with Fred Plotkin.
  • Explore the beauty of New York City at holiday time.

Day 2

Behind the Scenes at the Met and then, “Otello”

After a morning of leisure, gather at the hotel, where Fred Plotkin will talk about the history and meaning of the illustrious Metropolitan Opera since its founding in 1883. Then walk across the street for a backstage tour of the stunning Metropolitan Opera House. Have a pre-opera dinner at the Grand Tier Restaurant at the Met before taking your seats for Verdi’s “Otello.” The performance is scheduled to be conducted by Gustavo Dudamel in his first Met production and to star Sonya Yoncheva and Stuart Skelton.

Day 3

A New “La Traviata”

Spend the day exploring New York City on your own, perhaps taking in a Broadway matinee. Join your fellow opera lovers for dinner. This evening, attend a new production of Verdi’s “La Traviata.” The Met music director, Yannick Nézet-Séguin, is scheduled to conduct, and Diana Damrau, Juan Diego Flórez and Quinn Kelsey are scheduled to perform.

Day 4

New York Is Your Apple

Have brunch with your fellow opera lovers and then enjoy the day as you wish. The holiday lights are still up, and some of the crowds have gone, so the city is at its festive, beautiful best. Perhaps you want to visit any of New York’s amazing museums, go to a Broadway or Off Broadway show, or go shopping just as the post-holiday sales begin. Fred Plotkin, a native New Yorker, can help you plan your day and find some New York secrets.

Day 5

Opening Night for “Adriana Lecouvreur”

After a morning on your own, join Fred Plotkin as he discusses Francesco Cilea’s “Adriana Lecouvreur.” Dress up for this evening’s opening night of that opera, scheduled to be conducted by Gianandrea Noseda; Anna Netrebko and Piotr Beczala are scheduled to perform. Ring in the New Year with a festive dinner after the performance. Be prepared for some crowds as you return to your hotel, which is only about a mile from Times Square.

Day 6

A New Year and “The Magic Flute”

Fred Plotkin will review the performances you have seen so far and discuss issues affecting opera in New York and beyond. Like many companies, the Met is struggling with attendance, and is trying new ways to attract younger audiences. After the discussion, attend the Met’s holiday version, of “The Magic Flute” in English. Harry Bicket is scheduled to conduct with expected performances by Erin Morley, Ben Bliss and Nathan Gunn. Toast your operatic journey with a farewell dinner at The Leopard at des Artistes. It’s in the Hotel des Artistes, which was home to, among others, Norman Rockwell, Noel Coward and Isadora Duncan. The dining room features beautiful (and risqué) murals.

    Bernie Sanders Democrat United States senator from Vermont

    Calling himself a Democratic socialist, Mr. Sanders has served in Congress as an independent from Vermont for more than two decades. Far to the left in his politics and sometimes grumpy in his demeanor, he is asking voters to join him in what he has framed as a political revolution, appealing to progressives frustrated with big challenges like income inequality.

    • Islamic State

      Dec. 6, 2015

      “We have got to be as aggressive as we can in destroying ISIS, but we have to learn the lessons of the past, and that means we cannot do it alone.”

      Mr. Sanders, who highlights his opposition to the war in Iraq, has a deep reluctance to use of military force. He says that the United States should assemble an international coalition to fight the Islamic State, but that Muslim nations need to lead the fight and should provide the ground troops.

    • Syrian Refugees

      Nov. 14, 2015

      “What the magic number is, I don't know. Because we don’t know the extent of the problem. But I certainly think that the United States should take its full responsibility in helping” refugees.

    • Death Penalty

      Oct. 29, 2015

      “The state itself, in a democratic, civilized society, should itself not be involved in the murder of other Americans.”

      Mr. Sanders took to the Senate floor in October to make a case against the death penalty, offering an implicit contrast with Hillary Clinton.

    • Trans-Pacific Partnership

      Oct. 7, 2015

      “The Trans-Pacific Partnership is a continuation of disastrous trade policies which have cost this country millions of decent-paying jobs and have led to a race to the bottom.”

      Mr. Sanders has been an outspoken critic of the deal; he has highlighted his record of voting against trade agreements.

    • Syrian No-Fly Zone

      Oct. 3, 2015

      “I oppose, at this point, a unilateral American no-fly zone in Syria which could get us more deeply involved in that horrible civil war and lead to a never-ending U.S. entanglement in that region.”

      Mr. Sanders has warned about the possible consequences of a no-fly zone. “We do not want to make a very complex situation in Syria even worse,” he said.

    • Climate Change

      Aug. 30, 2015

      “Climate change is threatening this planet in horrendous ways.”

      Mr. Sanders has proposed taxing carbon emissions and eliminating tax breaks for fossil fuel companies. He has opposed the Keystone XL pipeline and Arctic drilling.

    • Health Care

      July 16, 2015

      “Health care, to my mind, is a right of all people.”

      Mr. Sanders has called for a Medicare-for-all single-payer health care system. He is also seeking to rein in the cost of prescription drugs and repeal the Cadillac tax on expensive employer health plans.

    • Gun Control

      July 5, 2015

      “I think I can bring us to the middle” on guns.

      Mr. Sanders has called for closing the gun-show loophole and banning assault weapons. In Congress, he voted for a law that shields gun manufacturers and dealers from lawsuits.

    • Immigration

      June 19, 2015

      “We must be aggressive in pursuing policies that are humane and sensible and that keep families together.”

      Mr. Sanders has called for a comprehensive approach and a path to citizenship, as well as expanded deportation protections, but is critical of guest-worker programs.

    • N.S.A.

      May 7, 2015

      “I voted against the Patriot Act every time, and it still needs major reform.”

      Mr. Sanders has been a critic of the government surveillance program and has argued for curtailing it. He is opposed to extending the Patriot Act and has pushed for stronger congressional oversight of how intelligence agencies use the law.

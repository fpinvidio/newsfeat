    Carlos Tejada is based in Hong Kong, and he oversees coverage of Asian business and economics for The New York Times. More

    Carlos Tejada is based in Hong Kong, and he oversees coverage of Asian business and economics for The New York Times.

    Mr. Tejada came to The Times from The Wall Street Journal, where he worked as a reporter and editor for two decades. He has worked in Dallas, New York and Beijing in addition to Hong Kong. He has written about real estate, the oil and gas industry, labor, steel and trade. As an editor, he has overseen coverage of the Chinese financial system, the country's stunning technology boom, and the Communist Party's role in shaping China's economic future.

    He has a bachelor’s degree in journalism from the University of Kansas, though his daughter tore up his diploma when she was three.

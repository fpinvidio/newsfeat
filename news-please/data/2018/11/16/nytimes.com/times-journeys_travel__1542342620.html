Join The New York Times, known for its coverage of dinosaurs, on this specially designed, six-day adventure to western Utah’s red rock country, for children 8 and older and their families. Dig in an active quarry, go behind the scenes in a paleontology laboratory, see the geologic wonders of the West and take a thrilling jet boat ride on the Colorado River.

This exclusive adventure explores one of the richest dinosaur areas in the American West. Children will dig alongside scientists in an active quarry where the remains of seven species of dinosaurs have been found. Families explore the entire dinosaur-hunting process, from prospecting to the preparation of fossils in the laboratory. Other activities include a boat ride and a barbecue on the banks of the Colorado River and options for hiking and other fun. This time in Utah provides a wonderful opportunity to engage and learn with your children or grandchildren.

During your journeys, your expert’s lecture topics will include:

  • How scientists have decoded dinosaur feathers, colors and sound
  • Prehistoric creatures preserved in amber
  • Dinosaur extinction and de-extinction (how scientists are trying to genetically reconstruct raptors from living chickens)
  • Unusual dinosaurs – from the giant sauropods to the bloodthirsty theropods, to the ones who swam and the ones who flew

Your journey includes:

  • On Tour Team: New York Times Expert, professional tour leader and local guides
  • Transfers: Group transfer upon arrival to Thanksgiving point. Airport shuttles from hotel to airport on last day.
  • Food & Drink: Most meals, plus welcome and farewell dinners. Bottled water throughout.
  • Accommodations: at 4- or 5- star hotels, including wifi where available
  • Logistics: Baggage handling, gratuities, taxes, and service charges
  • Activities/Fees: Included for all per itinerary
  • Other: Daily copy of The New York Times or Times Digest

Journey highlights include:

  • Go behind the scenes at a university paleontology laboratory for a special hands-on experience to learn how fossils are cleaned, identified and preserved.
  • Visit Cleveland-Lloyd Dinosaur Quarry, one of the most productive dig sites in the West.
  • Explore Arches National Park and learn about the unique geology of the area, which created the highest concentration of natural arches in the world.
  • Take a jet boat ride on the mighty Colorado River through spectacular red rock canyons.
  • Dig on your own for 250-million-year-old fossils, which you can bring home.
  • A New York Times expert will help you understand and learn as you have fun with the family.

Please note that pricing variations will depend on room occupancy and age of child. Call 855 698 7979 for details.

Day 1

Welcome to the Crossroads of the West

Arrive by midday at Salt Lake City International Airport. Depart as a group for Thanksgiving Point to tour the Museum of Ancient Life. This area includes 60 complete dinosaur skeletons, 50 hands-on exhibits and a carboniferous forest, the remnants of an ancient woods that has turned to coal. After this prehistoric experience, a great introduction to your adventure ahead, travel south and east to central Utah and the city of Price. Check into your hotel and have a welcome dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 2

Into the Labs

Check out of your hotel and head to Utah State University Eastern’s Prehistoric Museum for a tour and an orientation. Take a special guided tour of the university’s fossil lab, when you can have some hands-on experience with the preparation of real fossils. Learn how fossils are cleaned, identified and preserved. After a group lunch, visit Cleveland-Lloyd Dinosaur Quarry, one of the most productive dig sites in the West. See firsthand how a dig site is run and the painstaking process of removing the fossils from the surrounding rock. In the late afternoon travel to Moab, Utah’s adventure capital, your home for the next three nights. The evening is free for family time and dinner on your own.

Day 3

Arches National Park

This morning, depart for nearby Arches National Park. Learn about the unique geology of the area, which created the highest concentration of natural arches in the world: There are more than 2,000 named arches just within the park. Explore the most scenic points, including Delicate Arch, and have a picnic lunch. Later in the afternoon, visit Moab Giants, a dinosaur-themed attraction. Make your own tracks alongside state-of the art, life-size dinosaurs in the land they called home. Return to your hotel and spend the rest of the day and dinner at the pool, or with your family and new friends.

Day 4

The Mighty Colorado River

This morning enjoy all that Moab offers on your own. Some of the optional activities include horseback riding, river rafting, Hummer or Jeep tours, mountain biking, zip lining, climbing and canyoneering, hiking and much more. Later in the afternoon, take a beautiful drive to Dead Horse Point State Park to see the view from one of the West’s most scenic locations. You will see the expanse of Canyonlands National Park stretching to the south and the mighty Colorado River 2,000 feet below. Then take a jet boat ride on the mighty Colorado River through spectacular red rock canyons. As the sun dips in the west, the canyon colors come to life while you learn about the river’s ecosystem, geology and history. After a thrilling ride, a barbecue dinner will be served on the banks of the Colorado.

Day 5

Trilobite Dig

Check out of your hotel and travel to a quarry west of Delta to enjoy a day of digging for trilobites at U-Dig Fossils. The staff will show you how to dig for fossils. Then find your own 250-million-year-old fossils, which are plentiful here. Many of them are trilobites, ancestors of today’s lobsters and crabs, which look a bit like bedbugs or other insects. Everyone is guaranteed to unearth many fossils, and you can take home any you find. After a picnic lunch, return to Salt Lake City. Later, gather for a farewell dinner at a downtown restaurant. You’re sure to have lots to talk about.

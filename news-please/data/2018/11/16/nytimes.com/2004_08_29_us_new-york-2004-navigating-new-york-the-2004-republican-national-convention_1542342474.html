    NEW YORK 2004: NAVIGATING NEW YORK; The 2004 Republican National Convention

    By WILLIAM MCNULTY, JAMES BRONZAN and JOE BURGESSAUG. 29, 2004

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Planned Protests

    Disruptions can be expected along the protest routes during the convention.

    SUNDAY

    1. Say No to the Bush Agenda, United for Peace and Justice

    A national coalition of nearly 900 groups is planning a march at noon from north of 14th St. at Seventh Ave. past the convention site to Union Square Park. Organizers say it could attract 250,000 people.

    MONDAY

    2. March and Rally for Justice

    Sponsored by Still We Rise, a coalition of more than 50 nonprofit organizations, many of them New York-based groups serving people infected with H.I.V., immigrants, the poor and the homeless. The demonstration begins at noon at Union Square and proceeds up Eighth Avenue to Madison Square Garden.

    TUESDAY

    Direct Action Day

    An anarchist collective calling itself the A31 Action Coalition has organized a series of nonviolent civil disobedience events that will use parts of Midtown to stage demonstrations, without permits.

    WEDNESDAY

    3. Worker's Rally

    At 4 p.m., the New York City Central Labor Council of the A.F.L.-C.I.O., which represents nearly 400 New York City unions, is holding a rally on Eighth Avenue between 23rd and 30th Streets.

    4. Women's Rally

    At 7 p.m., the National Organization for Women has planned what could become the largest rally in Central Park during the convention. They anticipate up to 50,000 people will gather on the East Meadow to draw women's issues.

    5. The Line

    From 8:13 to 8:31 a.m., in a demonstration organized by People for the American Way; the Imagine Festival of Arts, Issues and Ideas; and the New York State A.F.L.-C.I.O., thousands of people will form a symbolic unemployment line from Wall Street to Madison Square Garden.

    Avoiding Penn Station

    Long Island Rail Road commuters may wish to transfer to the subway at station stops in Brooklyn and Queens as an alternative to traveling into Penn Station.

    Transfer to the 7 train, from the L.I.R.R. to Midtown Manhattan and Times Square can be made at the Woodside, Hunterspoint Ave. and Long Island City stations. Hunterspoint and Long Island City stations are rush-hour service only.

    Convenient connections to subway lines serving Lower and Midtown Manhattan can be made at the L.I.R.R. Flatbush Ave. Terminal. Transfer can be made to the 2, 3, 4, 5, B, D, M, N, R and Q trains.

    The L.I.R.R. Jamaica Hub offers connections to the J, Z and E trains. Also AirTrain service to Kennedy Airport.

    At the Garden

    Two entrances to Penn Station will remain open.

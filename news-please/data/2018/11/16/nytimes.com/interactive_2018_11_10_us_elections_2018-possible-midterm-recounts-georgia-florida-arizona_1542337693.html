    Full Florida Results ›

    Florida began the first full, statewide vote recount in its history on Saturday. The machine recount was triggered because the margin between Governor Rick Scott, the Republican nominee for Senate, and Senator Bill Nelson, the Democratic incumbent, was less than 0.5 percentage points.


    On Thursday, the results of the machine recount showed a margin of less than 0.25 percentage points, and the secretary of state ordered a manual recount. Election workers will examine “undervotes” or “overvotes” — ballots where optical scanning machines picked up no selection, or too many, for the race. The deadline for the manual recount is Nov. 18.


    Populous Palm Beach County was unable to complete the machine recount and had to revert to earlier vote totals. Mr. Nelson’s attorneys have asked that the county examine every ballot in a manual recount, not just the irregular votes.

    The margin in Florida’s governor race was also less than 0.5 percentage points in initial results, so a machine recount was triggered. The contest pitted Andrew Gillum, who is the mayor of Tallahassee, against Ron DeSantis, a Republican congressman who allied himself with President Trump.


    On Thursday, the machine recount showed a margin of 0.41 percentage points between Mr. DeSantis and Mr. Gillum, with Mr. DeSantis ahead.

    Full Georgia Results ›

    A federal judge on Monday blocked Georgia from certifying its election results before Friday at 5 p.m. Counties were required to submit results to the state by Tuesday, and the acting secretary of state had said she would certify the results on Wednesday. Georgia law requires a runoff in early December if neither candidate receives more than 50 percent of the vote. A recount is triggered if one candidate gets a majority but the margin between the two candidates is less than or equal to 1 percent.

    U.S. Senate in Arizona›

    Candidate Party Votes Pct.
    Kyrsten Sinema Democrat Dem. 1,097,321 49.68%
    Martha McSally Republican Rep. 1,059,124 47.96%
    Margin for Sinema +38,197 +1.73 pts.

    2,208,567 votes, 99% reporting (1,486 of 1,489 precincts)

    Full Arizona Results ›

    The Democrat, Representative Kyrsten Sinema, initially trailed the Republican, Representative Martha McSally. But as more mail ballots were counted, Ms. Sinema jumped into the lead. Her vote share kept growing until the Associated Press declared her the winner on Monday. A recount would have been triggered if the final margin between the candidates was less than or equal to one-tenth of 1 percent, but this is now unlikely because of the size of Ms. Sinema’s lead.

    [Ms. Sinema was declared the winner after a campaign in which she embraced solidly centrist positions.]

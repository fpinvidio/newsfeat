    9 Podcast Episodes Worth Discussing

    APRIL 5, 2017

    We're inviting diehard podcast fans and curious newcomers alike to join The New York Times Podcast Club, where we discuss one episode of one show each week. To get started, you can catch up with some of what we’ve listened to so far. Here are nine episodes we’ve discussed over the last year, and a quick explanation of why we chose them.

    1. From Outside Magazine
      1. ‘Outside Podcast’
      Science of Survival, Episode 01: ‘Frozen Alive’

      This episode was intriguing to us as a case study in how to blend interview, voiced readings and sound design to bring an archival print article to life as audio. And we were fascinated by the idea of an entire story told in second person.

      Listen on iTunes | Stitcher

    2. From NPR
      2. ‘Latino USA’
      #1508 – ‘A Day at the Bodega’

      In some ways the premise is dead simple: Spend an entire day at one bodega and see what happens. Is it enough to sustain an hour? And how does the tone and style of the episode change as one producer’s shift ends and another’s begins? We decided to listen with those questions in mind.

      Listen at LatinoUSA.org

    3. From Gimlet Creative and eBay
      3. ‘Open for Business’
      Episode 1: ‘The Entrepreneurial Mind’

      Many of us had listened to (or at least heard about) Panoply’s hit branded content show, ‘The Message.’ We were curious to hear how the sponsored-content form worked with something more journalistic.

      Listen on iTunes | Stitcher

    4. From Chicago Public Media
      4. ‘This American Life’
      Episode 562, ‘The Problem We All Live With’

      This episode about school desegregation, featuring Nikole Hannah-Jones, was already a favorite episode for a lot of us. Since Nikole is in the building — she’s a staff writer for The New York Times Magazine — we invited her to join us for the discussion. She confirmed what we suspected: It takes a LOT of time to make a reported radio story, and that’s coming from an investigative reporter who’s used to spending a long time on a project.

      Listen on This American Life’s website

    6. From Buzzfeed
      5. ‘Another Round’
      Episode 37: ‘The Room Where It Happens’ (With Valerie Jarrett)

      We asked Nikole to recommend the next show to discuss, and she suggested “Another Round.” We chose Heben and Tracy’s interview with Valerie Jarrett. Many of us who were regular listeners of the show had already been struck by it for how much it stood out from a typical interview with a White House advisor. The close runner-up episode was the interview with Hillary Clinton.

      Listen on iTunes | Stitcher

    7. From Radiotopia
      6. ‘Love + Radio’
      ‘The Living Room’

      An utterly captivating story about an urban voyeur that, bizarre as it was, ended up being the first episode in Podcast Club history to garner unanimously good feelings.

      Listen on iTunes | Stitcher

    8. From APM Reports
      7. ‘In the Dark’
      1: The Crime

      One reporter revisits a long-ago crime and tells the story week by week. Sounds like another “Serial” knock-off, right? Nope. Within the first minutes of the first episode it’s clear that this isn’t a typical whodunnit: That’s when they tell you the murderer’s name.

      Listen on iTunes | Stitcher

    10. From Panoply
      8. ‘Bad With Money With Gaby Dunn’
      ‘Jagged Little Bills’

      Lisa Tobin, The Times’s executive producer for audio, suggested this one because she thought it sounded unlike any show she’d heard before. We brought Ron Lieber, the “Your Money” columnist, to the discussion to get his take. Many of us loved the host’s charm and humor, but found the show frustrating from a journalistic and service perspective. Great if you want to be entertained. Not if you want to know how to be better with money.

      Listen on iTunes | Stitcher

    11. From WBEZ Chicago
      9. ‘Making Oprah’
      Episode 1: ‘No Strategy, No Plan, No Formula’

      One of the many aspects of this show that fascinated us was the structure. There aren’t many three-part podcasts. Also, Oprah!

      Listen on iTunes | Stitcher

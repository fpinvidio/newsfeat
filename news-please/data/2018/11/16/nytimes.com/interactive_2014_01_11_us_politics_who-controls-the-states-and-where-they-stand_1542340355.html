    Taking the Battle to the States

    By HAEYOUN PARK, JEREMY ASHKENAS and MIKE BOSTOCK JAN. 11, 2014

    Republicans or Democrats have single-party control of both the legislature and the governor’s office in 36 states, the most in six decades. Lawmakers in these states have been seeking to reshape government policy in recent years, from legalizing same-sex marriage to restricting labor unions. Some of these laws were passed after the rapid rise of single-party control in 2010; others have been in place for years. Below is a look at where states stand on some key issues.


    Notes: Nebraska has a unicameral, nonpartisan legislature. In New York and Washington, Democrats control the governor’s offices and have a numerical majority in both chambers of the legislatures. But in both states, one chamber is run by a coalition of Republicans and Democrats.

    *Same-sex marriages in Illinois will be allowed starting in June. In Utah, the state is appealing a federal court ruling striking down its ban on same-sex marriage. Pennsylvania and Wisconsin have both enacted voter ID laws, but the laws have not taken effect yet, pending court action.

    Sources: National Conference of State Legislatures; Guttmacher Institute; Kaiser Family Foundation; Department of Labor; National Employment Law Project; Movement Advancement Project; Human Rights Campaign

    Four-Star Restaurants in New York

    News about Four-Star Restaurants in New York, including commentary and archival articles published in The New York Times. More

    Only a handful of New York restaurants have been awarded four stars by the The New York Times, the highest designation in The Times’s rating system. The five restaurants that currently have four stars are Del Posto, Eleven Madison Park, Jean-Georges, Le Bernardin and Sushi Nakazawa, the most recent addition to the list, reviewed by Pete Wells in 2013.

    On Jan. 12, 2016, Per Se, which was awarded four stars by Frank Bruni in 2004 and by Sam Sifton in 2011, was reviewed by Mr. Wells, who gave it two stars. Daniel, which received four stars from William Grimes in 2001 and from Mr. Bruni in 2009, was re-reviewed in 2013 by Mr. Wells, who gave it three stars.

    2. RESTAURANT REVIEW

      Moving Ever Forward, Like a Fish

      ERIC RIPERT has been the executive chef of Le Bernardin for 18 years, and people sometimes ask him if he gets tired of cooking fish. Clearly these people have never eaten at Le Bernardin. No other restaurant in the city makes the simple cooking of fish (and the fish at Le Bernardin is cooked simply, when it is cooked at all) seem so ripe with opportunities for excitement. Some of the thrills are the hushed kind, like the way black garlic, pomegranate and lime support the crisp skin and white flesh of sautéed black bass. Others are scene-stealers, as when a white slab of steamed halibut is slowly surrounded by a crimson pool of beet sauce that, with crème fraîche stirred in, will turn the delirious pink of summer borscht.
      May 22, 2012

How Occupy Wall Street Turned Zuccotti Park Into a Protest Camp

Zuccotti Park, in Lower Manhattan, has been occupied since Sept. 17 by protesters in a group called Occupy Wall Street. The protesters have transformed the park into an operations center, as well as a place for some of the participants to live. The group says that it has no leaders and that major decisions are made by consensus in “general assembly” meetings. Here is how they have organized park in recent days. Related Article »

Sleeping Even during the day, people sleep wherever there is space for a sleeping bag. Since the mass arrests on Oct. 1 on the Brooklyn Bridge, about 500 people are in the park during the day and about 250 at night, according to the group.

Supplies Donated clothing, bedding, toothpaste, deodorant and other supplies are distributed here.

Medical About 25 medical volunteers, including lifeguards and doctors, are on rotation to provide 24-hour care.

Sanitation Location where people can volunteer to help clean the park.

Food The space is divided into two areas: one for food, one for drinks. The donated food has included fruit, vegetables, pizzas, cookies, pies and energy bars, with options for vegetarians.

Media Volunteers with laptop computers edit videos, write articles and update Web sites with information about the protest. Others create signs and handouts for promotion and recruiting.

Meeting area While spontaneous meetings occur throughout the park, “general assembly” meetings have been held here.

By BEDEL SAGET and ARCHIE TSE |

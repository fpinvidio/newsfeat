The 115 Men Who Will Choose the Next Pope

Cardinal electors from every region of the world will gather to choose the man to succeed Pope Benedict XVI.

Related: Inside the ConclaveTop Contenders to Lead the Roman Catholic Church
Continent

Europe is the most-represented continent, by a wide margin.

Country

Italy, the administrative center of the church, has the largest number of cardinals.

Appointer

Pope Benedict XVI appointed 67 of the cardinal electors; John Paul II appointed 48.

Age

Cardinal electors must be younger than 80 to participate in the conclave. The average age is 72.

By HANNAH FAIRFIELD, JON HUANG and AMANDA COX

Sources: Melissa J. Wilde, University of Pennsylvania; Holy See press office; Salvador Miranda, Florida International University

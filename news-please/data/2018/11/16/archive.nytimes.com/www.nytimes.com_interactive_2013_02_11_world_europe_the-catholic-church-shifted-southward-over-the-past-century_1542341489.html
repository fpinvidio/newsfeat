The Catholic Church Shifted Southward Over the Past Century

Jorge Mario Bergoglio, who was the archbishop of Buenos Aires, is the new pope. He will be called Francis and is the first non-European leader of the church in more than 1,200 years. Over the last century, much of the growth of the Roman Catholic Church has been outside Europe, and there are now more than 200 million more Catholics in Latin America than in Europe.

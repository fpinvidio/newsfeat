Top Contenders to Lead the Roman Catholic Church

Cardinals selecting the next pope are looking for someone with the charisma of Pope John Paul II and the grit to tackle the church’s scandals.

Top Contenders to Lead the Roman Catholic Church

Cardinals selecting the next pope are looking for someone with the charisma of Pope John Paul II and the grit to tackle the church’s scandals. Related: Inside the ConclaveThe 115 Men Who Will Choose the Next Pope

Timothy M. Dolan

Why he might be pope

He is relentlessly joyful and telegenic. Chosen by fellow bishops to lead their United States conference, he also served as rector at the American seminary in Rome.

Why he might not be pope

He does not speak Italian well, and his outsize personality is distinctively American. As archbishop in Milwaukee, he authorized payments to abusive priests to get them to step down.

Péter Erdö

Why he might be pope

A leader in the church in Europe, he has been at the forefront of dialogue with the Orthodox church. He has ties to African cardinals and is considered scholarly and pastoral.

Why he might not be pope

His age may be a drawback for those who do not want to see a repeat of John Paul II’s long papacy. He lacks a forceful public persona and may not be seen as a good enough communicator.

Sean P. O’Malley

Why he might be pope

A Capuchin Franciscan friar known for his humility, he has been tapped to repair dioceses riven by sexual abuse. He is known as a talented preacher, with a sense of comic timing; multilingual.

Why he might not be pope

He strikes some as remote. Although he sold the archbishop’s residence to raise money, Boston still faces financial challenges. Some say he was too slow to name abusive priests.

Marc Ouellet

Why he might be pope

A polyglot and a keen intellectual in the mold of Benedict XVI, he holds a critical Vatican post and knows how the hierarchy works; well versed in Latin American issues.

Why he might not be pope

He is not loved in Quebec, where, as archbishop, he said abortion was not justified even in rape cases. Some say he may lack the backbone – or the stomach – to tackle changes.

Leonardo Sandri

Why he might be pope

He is a widely traveled diplomat and knows the inner workings of the Curia. Born in Argentina to Italian parents, he is a bridge between old and new worlds.

Why he might not be pope

He has little pastoral experience. His closeness to the Curia could thwart efforts for change. The Italian news media have questioned his ties to a figure in a corruption inquiry.

Odilo Pedro Scherer

Why he might be pope

Backed by the Roman Curia, he has German ancestry but could become the first pope from the country with the most Catholics. Known as an administrator, he firmly advocates evangelism.

Why he might not be pope

He is only 63 and is seen by some as not charismatic enough to lead the church. Some note that he has been unable to stem the loss of members to evangelical movements in Brazil.

Angelo Scola

Why he might be pope

He is a fierce, media savvy intellectual, who peppers his writings with citations from Shakespeare and others like Jack Kerouac. His foundation is a platform for dialogue with Islam.

Why he might not be pope

He is Italian. He was involved for years with the Communion and Liberation movement, which has had close ties with Italian politicians. Some think his speeches are overly intellectual.

Luis Antonio G. Tagle

Why he might be pope

With a Chinese mother and roots in the Philippines, he would be a pope from the developing world. His charisma has been compared with John Paul II, and he is described as humble.

Why he might not be pope

He is one of the youngest cardinals and has no Vatican experience. Conservatives may consider his scholarly work too liberal.

An earlier version of this graphic misstated the country and age for Angelo Scola. He is from Italy, not Brazil, and he is 71, not 63.
